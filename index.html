Mission & Vision

Our mission is to empower individuals to achieve mental wellness and live fulfilling lives. We strive to reduce stigma surrounding mental health and promote access to quality care for all.

Our vision is to envision a society where everyone feels empowered to seek help for their mental health concerns and where mental wellness is recognized as essential to overall well-being.

Conditions that we treat....

Here are some mental health conditions that we treat for your mental wellness.



Depression

Persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities. Changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating.

Know more

Trauma

Trauma resulting from experiences such as accidents, abuse, or violence. Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares, hypervigilance, and avoidance of trauma-related triggers.

Know more

Sexuality

Understanding, affirmation, and support for diverse sexual orientations, gender identities, and relationships. Fostering a safe space for exploration and acceptance.

Know more

Stress

Adjustment Disorders, involving emotional or behavioral symptoms in response to stressful life events. Acute Stress Disorder, marked by severe anxiety and dissociative symptoms following a traumatic event.

Know more

OCD

Intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) to alleviate anxiety. Compulsions may include excessive cleaning, checking, or counting rituals.

Know more

Bi-polar Disorder

Episodes of mood swings ranging from depressive lows to manic highs. Fluctuations in energy levels, activity levels, sleep patterns, and judgment.

Know more

PTSD

Intrusive Thoughts like Flashbacks, nightmares, or distressing memories of the traumatic event. Avoidance of people, places, or activities that remind the individual of the trauma. Negative thoughts about oneself or the world, feelings of detachment, and difficulty experiencing positive emotion.

Know more

Anxiety

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), characterized by excessive worry and fear about everyday situations. Panic Disorder, marked by sudden and repeated episodes of intense fear or terror. Social Anxiety Disorder, involving overwhelming anxiety and self-consciousness in social situations.

Know more

ADHD

Persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning. Symptoms may present in childhood and continue into adulthood.

Know more

Addiction

Addiction is a complex condition where an individual becomes dependent on a substance or behavior, often leading to negative consequences in their life. It affects both the brain and behavior, making it difficult to stop engaging in the addictive behavior despite wanting to.

Know more

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia can cause symptoms like hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking, which can make it difficult for someone to distinguish between what is real and what isn't.

Know more

Insomnia

Insomnia is characterized by difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up too early and not being able to fall back asleep. This can lead to tiredness during the day and can impact overall health and quality of life.

Know more

At InnerWhispers, we are here to support your mental health, but we are not equipped to handle emergencies such as self-harm, suicidal thoughts, or severe mental health conditions like psychosis or schizophrenia. In these situations, immediate help from a professional is essential. If you or someone you know is in crisis, please reach out to the nearest hospital or emergency service for assistance. You can also contact a trusted psychiatrist, counselor, or therapist for urgent support. Involving a family member or close friend can provide additional comfort and help during this time.

Remember, seeking professional help promptly can make a significant difference, and you are not alone in this journey. Your safety and well-being are always the priority.
