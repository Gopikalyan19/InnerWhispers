Finding Calm Amidst the Storm.

Anxiety can feel overwhelming, but with the right support, you can regain control.

  • Emotional Impact: Constant worry, fear, or restlessness.
    Overthinking or feeling on edge.
  • Physical Impact:Rapid heart rate, shortness of breath, or dizziness.
    Muscle tension, headaches, or stomach problems.
  • Behavioral Impact: Avoidance of certain places, activities, or situations that trigger anxiety.
    Difficulty concentrating or making decisions.

What does Anxiety looks like?

Anxiety is a natural response to stress, but when it becomes excessive or persistent, it can interfere with daily life. People with anxiety may experience constant worry, fear, or nervousness. These feelings can be accompanied by physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat, sweating, or difficulty breathing.

  • Breathing Techniques: Slow, deep breathing exercises to activate the body's relaxation response.
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Reframing negative thoughts and learning coping skills.
  • Exercise: Physical activity to reduce stress and increase serotonin levels.
