Trauma impacts countless lives, leaving deep emotional and physical scars. Recognizing and understanding it is the first step toward recovery and resilience.

  • Emotional Impact: Anxiety, fear, anger, or numbness.
  • Physical Impact: Sleep disturbances,headaches.
  • Behavioral Impact:Avoidance, difficulty trusting others, or compulsive behaviors.

Trauma and the body

Trauma doesn’t just affect the mind; it deeply impacts the body as well. When someone experiences trauma, their body activates a “fight, flight, or freeze” response, releasing stress hormones like adrenaline and cortisol. This response can stay activated even long after the traumatic event, leading to physical symptoms like tension, aches, headaches, stomach problems, and fatigue. Trauma can also get “stored” in the body, causing people to feel disconnected or on edge without knowing why.
