Counselings that we provide

This is the process of providing guidance, support, and assistance to individuals who are experiencing personal, emotional, social, or psychological difficulties.

Counseling provides individuals with a supportive and non-judgmental space to explore their concerns, gain insight, and develop skills to overcome challenges and lead fulfilling lives. With the guidance of a trained counselor, individuals can learn to navigate life's difficulties with greater resilience, self-awareness, and empowerment.

Conditions that we treat....

Here are some mental health conditions that we treat for your mental wellness.

Depression

Persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities. Changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating.

Trauma

Trauma resulting from experiences such as accidents, abuse, or violence. Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares, hypervigilance, and avoidance of trauma-related triggers.

Sexuality

Understanding, affirmation, and support for diverse sexual orientations, gender identities, and relationships. Fostering a safe space for exploration and acceptance.

Stress

Adjustment Disorders, involving emotional or behavioral symptoms in response to stressful life events. Acute Stress Disorder, marked by severe anxiety and dissociative symptoms following a traumatic event.

OCD

Intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) to alleviate anxiety. Compulsions may include excessive cleaning, checking, or counting rituals.

Bi-polar Disorder

Episodes of mood swings ranging from depressive lows to manic highs. Fluctuations in energy levels, activity levels, sleep patterns, and judgment.

PTSD

Intrusive Thoughts like Flashbacks, nightmares, or distressing memories of the traumatic event. Avoidance of people, places, or activities that remind the individual of the trauma. Negative thoughts about oneself or the world, feelings of detachment, and difficulty experiencing positive emotion

Anxiety

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), characterized by excessive worry and fear about everyday situations. Panic Disorder, marked by sudden and repeated episodes of intense fear or terror. Social Anxiety Disorder, involving overwhelming anxiety and self-consciousness in social situations.

ADHD

Persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning. Symptoms may present in childhood and continue into adulthood.
